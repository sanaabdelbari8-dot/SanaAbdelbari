Eritrean Food

common food and dishes

When eating injera diners generally share food from a large tray placed in the centre of a low dining table. Numerous pieces of injera are layered on this tray and topped with various spicy stews. Diners break into the section of injera in front of them, tearing off pieces and dipping them into the stews.

what people said about eritrean food

People describe Eritrean food as flavorful, unique, and delicious, often highlighting the communal dining experience and the use of injera bread as both a plate and utensil. Praised for its diverse flavors, which can range from mild to spicy, it's also noted as a healthy and filling option, with many dishes that are vegan and vegetarian-friendly. While many enjoy the spongy, sour injera, some find the texture unusual.

Coffee ceremony

Eritrean coffee is a cultural ritual known as a coffee ceremony, which involves roasting, grinding, and brewing coffee in a traditional pot called a jebena. This ceremony is a vital symbol of hospitality and friendship, often accompanied by snacks like popcorn and taking place over several hours, with the coffee served in three stages: Awel, Kalai, and Baraka. The process is slow and deliberate, and the coffee itself is rich and often enjoyed without sugar, similar to an espresso.
